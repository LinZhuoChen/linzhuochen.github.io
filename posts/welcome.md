For a long time my writing has lived in two places: half-finished Notion pages I rarely revisit,
and Slack DMs to my advisor that vanish after a week. Neither is good for thinking.

So I'm starting this small writing space. It's not a Substack and it's not a research blog
in the formal sense — it's more like a public notebook. The bar to publish is **"would I
want to read this six months from now?"**, not "is this polished enough to defend?".

## What I plan to write about

A few rough buckets:

- **Research notes.** Half-formed ideas around 3D foundation models, world models, and
  feed-forward reconstruction. The kind of thing that's too long for a tweet and too
  unfinished for a paper.
- **Reading reactions.** When a paper genuinely changes how I think about a problem, I want
  to write down *why* before the feeling fades.
- **Tools & workflows.** Small things I build for myself — scripts, prompts, dotfiles —
  that other PhD students might find useful.
- **Occasional reflections.** Career-stage musings, especially the kind I wish someone had
  told me when I was deciding to do a PhD.

## What I won't do

I'm not going to set a posting cadence. Performative blogging is the fastest way to make
something stop being fun, and I already have enough deadlines.

## How this is built

The whole blog is a few hundred lines of CSS and a single Markdown renderer. Each post is
just a `.md` file in `/posts/`, and the homepage card list is hand-edited. No build step,
no CMS, no analytics. If I outgrow this, I'll switch — but for now, friction-free writing
beats every fancy stack I've tried.

If you find an error, an interesting paper, or just want to talk about 3D vision,
